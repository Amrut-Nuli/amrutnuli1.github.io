Dynamic Analysis of Automotive Front Transmission Axle
ICAPSM 2024
Final Year Project  ·  Presented at ICAPSM 2024

Comprehensive FEA and rotordynamic study of a front transmission shaft axle to identify critical speeds, assess vibrational mode stability, and enhance dynamic response by integrating hybrid bearing technologies.

SolidWorks ANSYS Workbench Modal Analysis Campbell Diagram Bearing Calculations Rotordynamics
  • Hybrid Bearings: Explored replacement of conventional angular contact ball bearings with non-contact magnetic bearings to reduce friction, energy loss, and wear.
  • FEA & Modal Analysis: SolidWorks 3D modelling + ANSYS Workbench static structural, modal analysis, and Campbell diagram stability analysis.
  • Field Validation: Integrated field survey data from authorized service centers to address real-world bearing seal degradation and contaminant ingress failures.
  • MAM Configuration: Magnetic-Angular-Magnetic hybrid identified as optimal — 18.38% reduction in peak mode deformation vs. conventional AAA baseline.
  • Frequency Optimization: Operating frequencies lowered by 4.59%, resulting in improved transmission efficiency.

Jig & Fixture Design for Precision Drilling
Manufacturing
Academic Project  ·  Process Planning & GD&T

Design and develop a process plan for a jig facilitating the drilling of three Ø7 mm holes on a shaft component, solving the challenge of precision hole location on cylindrical surfaces.

SolidWorks GD&T Process Planning Material Selection 2D Drafting
  • Functional Efficiency: 3-2-1 principle constrains all degrees of freedom — repeatable drilling within ±0.2 mm positional tolerance.

Autonomous Multi-Functional Cleaning Robot
Mechatronics
Academic Project  ·  Design & Fabrication

Design and fabricate a cost-effective autonomous mechatronic floor-cleaning robot capable of simultaneous dry vacuuming and wet mopping using Arduino-based control and ultrasonic obstacle avoidance.

SolidWorks Arduino IDE TinkerCAD C++ Bluetooth IoT Motor Sizing
